Switching Cost

Why users don't leave their current IME

Microsoft AI

Adoption is driven by inertia, not deliberate choice.

Most users inherited their IME through pre-installation or childhood exposure. No one shops for an IME.

THE LOCK-IN FACTOR

9-10

out of 10

Importance rating for personalized word library

Users develop a deeply personal relationship with their IME's vocabulary. Custom phrases like "jqxx" → "machine learning" represent years of personalization.

Implication: A new IME must offer seamless word library migration, or users won't switch.

FINDING 03

AI Features

What users want — and what they reject

Microsoft AI

FEATURE RECEPTION

STRONGEST POSITIVE

Translation

"I really trust Chinese-English translation. It can directly replace my text."

Key ask: AI-powered quality, tone options, editable output

POLARIZING

Candidate Bar Search

"Useful 1 in 10 times, distracting the other 9."

Design: Must be opt-in, not automatic

WEAKEST

Voice Input

"Talking to yourself in the office is strange. Typing is faster and more precise."

Barrier: Environmental + functional

Microsoft AI

THE PRINCIPLE

"Invisible

until invoked"

The IME should be a tool users reach for, not a voice that speaks unbidden. All AI features must be strictly user-initiated.

Microsoft AI

WHAT TO DO

Recommendations

Microsoft AI

Four Strategic Priorities

Core First

Migration

Clean+Smart

Privacy

Microsoft AI

01

Win on core typing first

Match Sogou's prediction accuracy before adding AI. Abbreviation expansion, personalized learning, trending vocabulary.

02

Build frictionless word library migration

Import from Sogou, Baidu, WeChat. This directly addresses the #1 reason users don't switch.

03

Position as "Clean + Smart"

Against Sogou's bloat and WeChat's limited features. Everything you need, nothing you don't.

04

Make privacy a headline feature

"Your typing stays on your device" — powerful in China where third-party IMEs are widely distrusted.

Microsoft AI
