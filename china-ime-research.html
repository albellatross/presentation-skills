Research Background

Understanding Chinese PC users' IME experiences, adoption drivers, and feature preferences

With AI integration accelerating across Chinese IMEs from Sogou, Baidu, and iFlytek, Microsoft is preparing a new IME specifically for the Chinese market.

Microsoft AI December 2025

Research Methodology

12 moderated 1-on-1 interviews conducted December 9-24, 2025 via online and offline sessions.

Participant mix: 9 male, 3 female | Ages 18-44 | 7 Sogou users, 3 MS default, 1 Windows IME, 1 WeChat IME

Roles: UX researchers, engineers, product managers, graduate students, media influencers, IT professionals

Expert interview: Former Sogou IME product manager (AI features & commercialization)

Criteria: All participants spend 5+ hours/week typing on desktop and use AI tools at least weekly

SECTION 02

Feature Evaluation

Microsoft AI December 2025

Translation: Strongest Positive Reception

FEATURE EVALUATION

Inline Chinese-to-English translation received broadly positive feedback

Key expectations: AI-powered quality (not basic machine translation), tone/formality options, editability before insertion

User quote: "I really trust Chinese-English translation. It can directly replace my text — I don't need an intermediate confirmation step." — P12

Recommendation: Launch with multi-tone translation (casual, professional, academic) and support both typing-mode and reading-mode translation

Microsoft AI December 2025

Bing AI Search: Selection-Based Wins

Candidate bar search was polarizing — "useful 1 in 10 times, distracting the other 9" (P09). Must be opt-in, not automatic.

Selection-based search received more positive feedback — inherently user-initiated, occurs in reading context, feels less intrusive.

Design principle: "The intent should be determined at the beginning" — users want explicit triggers, not automatic suggestions.

Recommendation: Invest heavily in selection-based search; make candidate bar search opt-in only with subtle indicator.

Microsoft AI December 2025

AI INTEGRATION PRINCIPLE

"Invisible until invoked"

Users want AI assistance that is contextual, lightweight, and strictly user-initiated. The IME should be a tool users reach for, not a voice that speaks unbidden.

Strategic Recommendations

Core Typing

Win on prediction accuracy first. Ensure abbreviation expansion, personalized word learning, and trending vocabulary match or exceed Sogou before adding AI features.

Migration

Build and prominently promote a word library import tool supporting Sogou, Baidu, and WeChat to lower switching costs.

Positioning

"Clean + Smart": OS-integrated, no installation, no bloat, privacy-respecting, AI-powered where it matters. Position against Sogou's bloat and WeChat's limited features.

Privacy

Make privacy a headline feature: "Your typing stays on your device" — a powerful message in China where third-party IMEs are widely distrusted.
